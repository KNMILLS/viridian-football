## 2 Mapping schemes to player labels

To ensure that personnel fits drive play‑calling and AI decision‑making, map the player labels to the schemes and concepts above:

- **West Coast:** emphasises **possession receivers**, **slot receivers**, versatile **receiving backs** and **move/receiving tight ends**.  **Game Managers** and **Dual‑Threat QBs** with accuracy excel.  **Tenacious Blockers** and **Ath Blocker** traits help in the power run game.
- **Air Coryell/Vertical:** requires **Deep Threat** and **Speed Receiver** labels, **Gunslinger** quarterbacks with strong arms and willingness to throw deep, and offensive lines with **Tenacious Blocker** traits.  **Clutch QBs** and **Clutch Kickers** may be valuable in big‑play situations.
- **Run & Shoot:** thrives with **All‑Purpose RBs** and **Dual‑Threat QBs**, **Slot Receivers**, **Speed Receivers**, **Receiving TEs**, and **Film Geek** or **Perceptive** players who adjust routes on the fly.  **Workout Fanatic** and **Pro Bloodline** labels improve learning efficiency in this complex system.
- **Spread (Air Raid):** emphasises vertical speed; pairs well with **Deep Threat**, **Scat Back**, **Speed Rusher** and **Coverage LB** labels.  **Hybrid LB** and **Zone Corner** are needed on defense.  The **Athlete**, **All‑Purpose RB**, and **Dual‑Threat** traits are important on offense.
- **Spread Option:** demands **Dual‑Threat** quarterbacks, **Bruiser** running backs for inside zone, **Scat Backs** for outside runs, and **Hybrid LB** defenders who can handle misdirection.  **Box Safety** and **Zone Corner** help with run fits and option responsibilities.
- **Smashmouth Spread:** pairs spread formations with power running, so **Bruiser** backs, **Tenacious Blocker** linemen and **Blocking TEs** are essential.  **Slot Receivers** still contribute, but the run sets up play‑action.
- **Pro‑Style Spread:** balanced attack; values versatility.  **All‑Purpose RB**, **Possession WR**, **Slot Receiver**, **Power Runner** and **Power Kicker** fit well.
- **4‑3 defense:** uses **Press Corner**, **ShutDown Corner**, **Ball Hawk**, **Speed Rusher**, **Thumper** and **Coverage LB** traits.  **Box Safety** supports the run.
- **3‑4 defense:** emphasises **Hybrid LB**, **Bull Rusher**, **Speed Rusher**, **Nose Tackle** and **Zone Corner** traits.  **Centerfielder** safeties patrol deep zones while linebackers generate pressure.

## 3 Calibrating relationship and cohesion formulas

To reflect the influence of cohesion on performance, the engine will derive position‑group cohesion scores and calibrate their effects using historical data.

1. **Data collection:** gather public NFL data sets on offensive‑line continuity (number of games started together), quarterback‑receiver target share, secondary continuity and associated performance metrics such as sack rate, passer rating, yards per attempt and coverage success.  Use seasons with minimal injuries and stable coaching staffs to reduce confounding variables.
2. **Regression analysis:** run linear or nonlinear regressions to estimate how changes in continuity and trust correlate with performance.  For example, regress sack rate against offensive‑line continuity while controlling for quarterback pressure rate; regress completion percentage on the percentage of targets to the top receiver pairings.  Use the coefficients to scale the cohesion bonus or penalty in the simulation.
3. **Scaling effects:** convert regression coefficients into game‑engine modifiers.  For example, if each additional game of line continuity reduces sack rate by 0.5%, a fully cohesive offensive line could receive a 5% pass‑protection bonus, while a shuffled line incurs a penalty.  Similarly, high receiver‑QB cohesion could add a completion‑percentage bonus and reduce interception risk.
4. **Position‑group weighting:** weight cohesion effects according to interaction frequency: QB–WR/TE (highest), offensive line, secondary, linebacker unit and running backs.  These weights adjust how strongly cohesion modifiers affect play outcomes.
5. **Iterative tuning:** after implementing the initial formulas, run large‑scale Monte Carlo simulations to observe the distribution of outcomes.  Adjust coefficients to ensure realistic variance and emergent narratives.  During beta testing, gather player feedback to fine‑tune the perceived impact of cohesion.

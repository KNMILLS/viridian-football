# ADR-0002: Fatigue Model Selection
**Document ID**: ADR-0002  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Last Updated**: 2024-12-19  
**Status**: Active

## Context

The USE Engine requires a realistic fatigue modeling system that accurately represents how player performance degrades over time due to physical exertion, contact, and cumulative wear. The fatigue system must integrate with body state tracking, injury risk assessment, and CPU GM decision-making to provide authentic gameplay that reflects real-world football dynamics.

Key forces at play:
- **Realism vs. Playability**: Fatigue must significantly impact performance without making the game unenjoyable
- **Complexity vs. Performance**: Detailed fatigue modeling vs. real-time simulation requirements
- **Individual vs. Team**: Fatigue affects individual players but must scale to full team management
- **Short-term vs. Long-term**: In-game fatigue vs. season-long cumulative effects
- **Contact vs. Non-contact**: Different types of exertion have different fatigue impacts

The research emphasizes the importance of player development, morale, injuries, and fatigue realism【message_idx†source】, requiring a fatigue system that supports dynamic player narratives and realistic team management decisions.

## Decision

We will implement a two-timescale fatigue model with contact load integration:

1. **Acute Fatigue (F_acute)**: In-game fatigue that accumulates during plays and recovers between plays
2. **Cumulative Fatigue (F_cum)**: Season-long fatigue that accumulates across games and recovers during off-weeks
3. **Contact Load Integration**: Fatigue accumulation weighted by collision severity and body state
4. **Environmental Factors**: Heat, altitude, and game conditions modify fatigue rates
5. **Recovery Curves**: Non-linear recovery that reflects real-world physiology

The model will use normalized fatigue values (0-1) with explicit formulas for accumulation, recovery, and performance impact.

## Alternatives

**Alternative 1: Single-Timescale Linear Model**
- Description: Use a single fatigue value that accumulates linearly and recovers linearly
- Pros: Simple implementation, easy to understand and validate
- Cons: Unrealistic, doesn't capture different fatigue types, poor long-term modeling
- Why Rejected: Insufficient realism for authentic football simulation

**Alternative 2: Complex Physiological Model**
- Description: Implement detailed physiological modeling including muscle fatigue, cardiovascular fatigue, and neural fatigue
- Pros: Maximum realism, scientifically accurate
- Cons: Extremely complex, difficult to validate, performance overhead
- Why Rejected: Overly complex for current requirements and team capabilities

**Alternative 3: Event-Based Fatigue**
- Description: Fatigue accumulates only from specific events (tackles, sprints, etc.)
- Pros: Clear cause-effect relationships, easy to balance
- Cons: Misses continuous exertion, doesn't model cumulative effects
- Why Rejected: Incomplete modeling of real-world fatigue dynamics

**Alternative 4: Statistical Fatigue**
- Description: Use statistical models based on historical NFL data
- Pros: Data-driven, potentially accurate
- Cons: Requires extensive data, may not capture individual variation
- Why Rejected: Lack of comprehensive data and need for individual player modeling

**Alternative 5: Simplified Binary Fatigue**
- Description: Players are either "fresh" or "tired" with simple state transitions
- Pros: Very simple, fast computation
- Cons: Unrealistic, lacks nuance, poor integration with other systems
- Why Rejected: Insufficient depth for realistic simulation

## Consequences

**Positive Consequences:**
- **Realism**: Two-timescale model accurately reflects real-world fatigue patterns
- **Integration**: Contact load integration provides realistic injury risk modeling
- **Scalability**: Normalized values support efficient computation across full team
- **Validation**: Explicit formulas enable comprehensive testing and validation
- **Flexibility**: Environmental factors allow for varied game conditions

**Negative Consequences:**
- **Complexity**: Two-timescale model requires careful parameter tuning
- **Performance**: Additional computation for fatigue calculations
- **Balance**: Fatigue effects must be carefully balanced to maintain enjoyable gameplay
- **Validation**: Complex model requires extensive testing across different scenarios

**Neutral Consequences:**
- **Learning Curve**: Team must understand fatigue model parameters and effects
- **Maintenance**: Fatigue formulas may need adjustment based on gameplay feedback
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation required for fatigue system
